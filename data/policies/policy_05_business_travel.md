# Business Travel Policy

**Policy ID:** TTB-FIN-002  
**Effective Date:** February 1, 2025  
**Owner Department:** Finance Operations  

## 1. Booking Guidelines and Travel Portal
All business travel at ttb Policy Assistant Demo Bank must be authorized and booked in accordance with this policy to manage costs and ensure staff safety.
* **Mandatory Portal:** All flights, rail journeys, and hotel stays must be booked through the official ttb Corporate Travel Portal. Self-booking via third-party consumer sites is prohibited and will not be reimbursed.
* **Advance Booking Windows:** To secure optimal pricing, domestic travel must be booked at least 14 days in advance, and international travel must be booked at least 30 days in advance of the departure date.

## 2. Flight Class and Cabin Standards
* **Domestic Flights:** All domestic flights (under 5 hours flight duration) must be booked in Economy Class.
* **International Flights:** Flights exceeding 5 hours but under 8 continuous hours must be booked in Premium Economy (if available) or Economy Class.
* **Business Class:** Business class travel is strictly restricted to international flights where the scheduled continuous flight time exceeds 8 continuous hours. Multi-segment flights where no single segment exceeds 8 hours do not qualify for Business Class.
* **First Class:** First Class travel is strictly prohibited for all employees under all circumstances.

## 3. Lodging and Hotel Caps
Hotel bookings must use preferred partner hotels listed in the travel portal. Nightly rates are capped as follows:
* **Standard Locations:** Max $250.00 USD per night (before taxes).
* **High-Cost Cities:** Nightly rates are capped at $400.00 USD in designated High-Cost Cities, which include New York City, London, Tokyo, Paris, Geneva, and Singapore.
* **Conference Lodging:** If attending an approved industry conference, staff may book the official conference hotel even if it exceeds the standard caps, provided they get pre-approval.

## 4. Meals and Incidentals Per Diem
The bank pays a daily per diem allowance to cover meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner) and incidentals. Receipts are not required for per diem claims, but the travel must be registered in the portal:
* **Domestic Per Diem:** Capped at $75.00 USD per day.
* **International Per Diem:** Capped at $120.00 USD per day.
* **Deductions:** The per diem must be reduced by 25% if lunch or dinner is provided by a client, conference, or included in the hotel booking.

## 5. Approval Workflows
* **Domestic Travel:** Requires approval from the immediate Line Manager prior to booking.
* **International Travel:** Requires approval from both the Line Manager and the Department Head, and final registration with the Finance Operations Travel Team.
* **Group Travel:** If more than 4 employees from the same department are traveling to the same event, the travel plan must be pre-approved by the Head of Finance Operations to manage corporate risk.

## 6. Exceptions and Escalation
Exceptions to flight class limits, booking windows, or hotel caps require written justification and sign-off from the Head of Finance Operations prior to booking. If emergency travel is required with less than 24 hours' notice, bookings can be made via the Travel Portal's Emergency Hotline with subsequent manager approval.
