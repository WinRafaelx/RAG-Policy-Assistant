# AML Escalation Summary

**Policy ID:** TTB-COMP-002  
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2025  
**Owner Department:** Compliance Operations  

## 1. Objective and System Monitoring
This summary outlines the processes for identifying, investigating, and escalating potential money laundering, terrorist financing, and sanctions violations at ttb Policy Assistant Demo Bank.
* **Automated Screening:** All automated transactions exceeding $10,000.00 USD (or local currency equivalent) are screened by the automated AML Monitoring system. Any transaction matching suspicious patterns will generate an AML Alert.

## 2. Alert Review and Triage Timelines
* **Triage Window:** Suspicious Activity Alerts must be reviewed and triaged by an AML Analyst within 48 hours of generation.
* **Resolution:** The analyst must either dismiss the alert as a false positive (with documented rationale) or escalate the alert to the AML Investigations Team.
* **Documentation:** All notes, customer files, and transaction lists reviewed during triage must be attached to the alert case file.

## 3. Internal Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs)
* **Filing Deadline:** If any staff member (including branch staff or relationship managers) identifies a transaction indicating potential money laundering, they must file an internal Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) within 24 hours of identifying the activity.
* **No Transactions Blocked:** Filing an internal STR does not require blocking the transaction immediately, unless it triggers sanctions rules.

## 4. Anti-Tipping Off Prohibition
* **Strict Prohibition:** Under federal anti-money laundering laws, it is strictly prohibited to inform, hint, or alert a customer that they are under AML investigation or that an STR has been filed.
* **Consequences:** Tipping-off a customer carries a penalty of immediate termination of employment at ttb and referral to law enforcement for criminal prosecution.

## 5. High-Risk Escalations and Sanctions
* **Investigations Escalation:** Alerts escalated during triage are routed to the AML Investigations Team, who have 10 business days to complete a formal review.
* **Sanctioned Entities:** If a transaction or account name matches a designated sanctioned entity list (e.g., OFAC, UN sanctions), the transaction must be blocked immediately.
* **Sanction Escalation Deadline:** Sanctions matches must be escalated to the Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) within 1 hour of alert detection.

## 6. External Reporting Approval
The filing of an official external STR to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) is a legal action. It requires a detailed case file prepared by AML Investigations and formal sign-off from the Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) before submission.
