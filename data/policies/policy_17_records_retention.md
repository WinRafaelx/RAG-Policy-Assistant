# Records Retention Policy

**Policy ID:** TTB-COMP-003  
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2025  
**Owner Department:** Compliance Operations  

## 1. Purpose and Scope
This policy establishes the retention periods, disposal methods, and storage requirements for all documents, records, and data assets at ttb Policy Assistant Demo Bank. Proper retention ensures compliance with tax laws, financial regulations, and litigation readiness.

## 2. Retention Schedules
All records must be retained in accordance with the following schedules:
* **Customer Transaction Records:** Records of transactions, account statements, wire transfers, and deposit slips must be retained for a minimum of 7 years from the transaction date.
* **KYC and Onboarding Records:** Customer identity verification files, corporate structures, and KYC onboarding profiles must be retained for 10 years after the customer account is closed.
* **Human Resources Records:** Employee files, contracts, and performance evaluations must be retained for 7 years following employee termination.
* **Corporate Governance Records:** Board of Directors meeting minutes, audit reports, and corporate charters must be retained permanently.

## 3. Disposal and Shredding Standards
* **Secure Disposal:** At the end of the retention period, records must be destroyed in a secure manner to prevent data leaks.
* **Shredding Specifications:** Physical records containing Confidential or Restricted information must be shredded using a secure cross-cut shredder with a maximum shred size of 4mm x 40mm.
* **Certificate of Destruction:** Disposal of physical or digital records must be documented with a signed Certificate of Destruction, which must be archived in the compliance library.

## 4. Destruction Approvals
Before destroying any records, the business unit must submit a Destruction Request Form. The request requires written sign-off from:
1. The business unit's Department Manager.
2. The Compliance Operations Records Manager.

## 5. Legal Hold Framework
* **Legal Hold Definition:** A process suspending the destruction of records due to active, pending, or anticipated litigation or regulatory audit.
* **Authority to Issue:** Legal holds can only be issued and managed by the General Counsel.
* **Compliance:** Once a Legal Hold is issued, all records covered by the hold must be secured and cannot be destroyed under any circumstances, overriding standard retention timelines.

## 6. Policy Violations
Deleting or shredding records that are subject to an active Legal Hold or before their standard retention period expires is a violation. Suspected violations will be escalated to the Head of Compliance Operations.
