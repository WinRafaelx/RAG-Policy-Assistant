# Incident Reporting Policy

**Policy ID:** TTB-SEC-005  
**Effective Date:** November 15, 2024  
**Owner Department:** Information Security  

## 1. Objectives and Scope
This policy defines the procedures for identifying, classifying, and reporting information security and IT system incidents at ttb Policy Assistant Demo Bank. Timely reporting is essential to minimize financial losses, comply with banking regulations, and protect customer assets.

## 2. Incident Severity Classifications
Incidents are categorized into three severity levels based on their impact:
* **Severity 3 (Low):** Minor issues impacting a single department or non-critical system, with no impact on customers or data.
* **Severity 2 (Medium):** Issues impacting multiple departments, partial degradation of customer-facing portals, or isolated security events with no confirmed data leakage.
* **Severity 1 (High):** Critical incidents. This includes any confirmed security breach impacting core payment systems, unauthorized exposure of customer PII, or IT system downtime exceeding 15 minutes for core services (e.g., mobile banking, ATM network).

## 3. Notification Windows for Severity 1 Incidents
To comply with regulatory standards and activate emergency protocols:
* **20-Minute Notification:** The Incident Commander must notify the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) and the Executive Leadership Team within 20 minutes of declaring a Severity 1 incident.
* **Regulatory Notification:** If the incident involves a potential data breach of customer records, Compliance Operations must notify the Central Bank regulator within 72 hours of discovery.

## 4. Incident Management and CIRT Activation
* **Low & Medium Management:** Managed by the IT Service Desk and standard system administration teams in accordance with IT support SLAs.
* **Severity 1 Trigger:** Declaring a Severity 1 incident automatically triggers the activation of the Cyber Incident Response Team (CIRT) and the Crisis Management Committee (CMC). The CIRT will coordinate all technical containment and remediation efforts.

## 5. Post-Incident Review Requirements
* **PIR Submission:** A formal Post-Incident Review (PIR) report must be completed and submitted to the Technology Risk team within 5 business days of incident closure.
* **PIR Contents:** The report must detail the root cause, timeline of events, containment actions, impact analysis, and permanent remediation steps to prevent recurrence.

## 6. Public Communications and Approvals
Staff are strictly prohibited from discussing any IT or security incident with external parties, including the media. All public statements, press releases, or notifications to customers must be pre-approved in writing by both the General Counsel and the Head of Corporate Communications.
