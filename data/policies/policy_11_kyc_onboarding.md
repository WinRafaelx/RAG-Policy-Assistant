# KYC Onboarding Summary

**Policy ID:** TTB-COMP-001  
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2025  
**Owner Department:** Compliance Operations  

## 1. Policy Objective
This document outlines the standard operating procedures and regulatory requirements for Know Your Customer (KYC) onboarding for new retail and corporate customers at ttb Policy Assistant Demo Bank. Verification of customer identity is mandatory before establishing any banking relationship.

## 2. Retail Onboarding Verification
To onboard a new retail customer, KYC Analysts must verify identity using two primary government-issued documents:
1. **Primary ID:** A valid, unexpired Passport.
2. **Secondary ID:** A National Identification Card or a Driver's License.
* **Proof of Address:** A utility bill or bank statement from another registered financial institution issued within the last 90 days is required to verify the residential address.

## 3. Corporate Onboarding and Beneficial Ownership
For corporate customers, the bank must conduct deep due diligence on the entity structure:
* **Beneficial Owner Threshold:** KYC must identify and verify the identity of all Beneficial Owners holding more than 10% of the company's voting shares or equity.
* **Required Corporate Docs:** Corporate registry filings, articles of association, and a board resolution authorizing the account opening must be provided.

## 4. Quality and Timeline Standards
* **Document Scanning:** All physical KYC documents must be scanned and uploaded in high resolution (minimum 300 DPI) to the KYC Document Management System.
* **Upload Deadline:** Scans must be uploaded within 24 hours of customer submission.
* **Data Verification:** The customer's legal name, date of birth, and ID numbers must match the uploaded documents exactly.

## 5. Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
* **PEP Definition:** A Politically Exposed Person is any individual who holds or has held a prominent public function, or their close family members.
* **Risk Tier:** PEPs are automatically classified as High-Risk customers.
* **Enhanced Due Diligence:** PEPs must undergo Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) at onboarding. PEP profiles and transaction histories must be reviewed and updated annually by the Compliance team.

## 6. Approvals and Escalations
* **Standard Onboarding:** Processed and approved by a KYC Analyst.
* **High-Risk Onboarding (including PEPs):** Requires sign-off from the KYC Team Lead and final approval from the Head of Compliance Operations.
* **Discrepancy Escalation:** If verification documents contain discrepancies that cannot be resolved within 5 business days, the application must be escalated to the AML Operations Team for a security review.
