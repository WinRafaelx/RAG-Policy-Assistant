# IT Acceptable Use Policy

**Policy ID:** TTB-SEC-001  
**Effective Date:** November 15, 2024  
**Owner Department:** Information Security  

## 1. Authorized Use of Bank Systems
This policy establishes guidelines for the acceptable, secure, and ethical use of all information technology (IT) resources, systems, networks, and devices provided by ttb Policy Assistant Demo Bank.
* **Corporate Devices Only:** Staff must only access bank networks and internal systems using ttb-issued corporate devices.
* **BYOD Prohibition:** The use of personal computers, personal tablets, or personal mobile phones (Bring Your Own Device - BYOD) to connect directly to the corporate network or access internal applications is strictly prohibited.

## 2. Personal Use Thresholds
* **Reasonable Personal Use:** Limited personal use of ttb devices (such as checking news, checking personal email, or online banking) is permitted, provided it does not interfere with the employee's duties or network performance.
* **Time Limitation:** Personal usage of ttb devices must be kept to a minimum and must not exceed a cumulative total of 30 minutes per working day.
* **Prohibited Content:** Under no circumstances may ttb devices be used to access, download, or distribute inappropriate, illegal, offensive, or high-risk content (e.g., gambling, pornography, file-sharing sites).

## 3. Software Standards and Installation
* **Authorized Software:** Employees are prohibited from downloading or installing unauthorized software on any ttb device.
* **Approved Source:** All software applications must be installed from the pre-approved ttb Software Center.
* **Unlisted Software:** If an employee requires software not listed in the Software Center, they must submit a request via the IT Service Portal for review by the Technology Risk team before installation.

## 4. External Storage and Media Restrictions
* **USB Ports Blocked:** To prevent data leaks and malware infections, all USB and external storage ports on ttb laptops and desktops are blocked at the hardware and operating system level.
* **Secure USB Exception:** If an employee has a legitimate business need to transfer data via USB, they must request a secure, BitLocker-encrypted USB drive issued directly by the Information Security team.
* **Personal Drives:** The connection of personal USB flash drives, external hard drives, or personal mobile devices for file transfer is strictly blocked and logged.

## 5. Monitoring and Privacy Notice
* **No Expectation of Privacy:** All data created, transmitted, received, or stored on ttb IT systems is the property of ttb Policy Assistant Demo Bank. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using bank-provided IT systems.
* **Active Monitoring:** Information Security continuously monitors and audits network traffic, email communications, and device activities for security incidents and policy compliance.

## 6. Violations and Escalations
Non-compliance with this policy may result in immediate revocation of system access and disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Suspected policy violations or security compromises must be escalated immediately to the Information Security Incident Response Team (ISIRT).
