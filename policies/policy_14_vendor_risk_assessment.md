# Vendor Risk Assessment Policy

**Policy ID:** TTB-PROC-001  
**Effective Date:** April 1, 2025  
**Owner Department:** Procurement Risk  

## 1. Pre-Contract Assessment Scope
This policy establishes the risk-based framework for evaluating, onboarding, and managing third-party vendors at ttb Policy Assistant Demo Bank.
* **VRA Mandatory:** All prospective vendors must undergo a formal Vendor Risk Assessment (VRA) before contract execution or accessing any bank systems. No software purchases or service agreements may bypass this assessment.

## 2. Vendor Risk Tiers
Vendors are classified into one of four risk tiers based on the type of services provided and the data accessed:
* **Tier 1 (Critical):** Vendors that host or access Restricted customer data, or support critical banking services (e.g., core cloud hosting).
* **Tier 2 (High):** Vendors accessing Confidential data or supporting important but non-core operational systems.
* **Tier 3 (Medium):** Vendors with limited system access and no access to customer data.
* **Tier 4 (Low):** Vendors providing general business services with no network or data access (e.g., office catering).

## 3. Compliance and Security Documentation
* **SOC 2 Type II Requirement:** Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors must provide a valid SOC 2 Type II report. The report must have been issued within the last 12 months and cover a testing period of at least 6 months.
* **Financial Health Check:** Procurement Risk will conduct a financial viability audit for all Tier 1 vendors.

## 4. Risk Reassessment Cycles
To ensure ongoing compliance, vendors are subject to periodic risk reviews:
* **Tier 1 (Critical) Reassessment:** Must be reassessed annually.
* **Tier 2 and Tier 3 Reassessment:** Must be reassessed every 2 years.
* **Tier 4 Reassessment:** Reassessed only upon contract renewal or significant scope changes.

## 5. Onboarding Approvals and Workflows
* **Tier 4 (Low) Onboarding:** Requires approval from the purchasing department's Manager.
* **Tier 3 (Medium) Onboarding:** Requires approval from the Department Head and Procurement Risk.
* **Tier 1 (Critical) & Tier 2 (High) Onboarding:** Requires formal risk sign-off from the Procurement Risk Lead and final approval from the Head of Technology Risk.

## 6. Exceptions and Risk Waivers
If a business unit needs to onboard a vendor that does not meet the security standards (e.g., lacks a SOC 2 report), they must submit a formal Risk Waiver request. The waiver must document compensating controls, be reviewed by the Technology Risk team, and be approved by the Vendor Risk Committee.
