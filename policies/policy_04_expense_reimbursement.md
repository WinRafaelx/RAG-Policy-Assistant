# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Policy ID:** TTB-FIN-001  
**Effective Date:** February 1, 2025  
**Owner Department:** Finance Operations  

## 1. Policy Statement and Timelines
This policy defines the standards, rules, and procedures for submitting business-related expense claims at ttb Policy Assistant Demo Bank. All employees are required to act responsibly and exercise prudent judgement when spending bank funds.
* **Submission Deadline:** All expense reimbursement claims must be submitted in the corporate expense management system within 30 calendar days of the date the expense was incurred. Late submissions will be rejected.

## 2. Receipt and Documentation Requirements
* **Receipt Threshold:** Receipts are mandatory for all individual transactions exceeding $25.00 USD (or local currency equivalent). Itemized receipts showing the merchant name, date, items purchased, and tax breakdown must be provided.
* **Credit Card Slips:** Credit card charge slips alone are not accepted as valid receipts, as they do not show the itemized breakdown of goods purchased.
* **Missing Receipts:** For expenses under $25.00 USD where a receipt is unavailable, the employee must provide a detailed written explanation in the system.

## 3. Expense Categories and Spending Thresholds
* **Client Entertainment:** Business meals and client entertainment are permitted only when a client or external business partner is present. The maximum allowed expense is capped at $150.00 USD per attendee (including the ttb host employee), up to a maximum total cap of $1,000.00 USD per event.
* **Personal Mileage:** When employees are authorized to use their personal vehicles for bank business (excluding daily commuting to their primary office), they can claim mileage at a rate of $0.65 USD per mile. The claim must include the starting and ending addresses and the total mileage.
* **Business Subscriptions:** Subscriptions to professional publications or software must be billed to the department cost center and are capped at $200.00 USD per employee per year.

## 4. Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The bank will not reimburse personal or unauthorized expenses. Examples of non-reimbursable items include:
* Daily commuting costs to the primary office.
* Hotel room upgrades, minibar items, and in-room movie rentals.
* Traffic violations, parking fines, and speeding tickets.
* Alcohol, unless part of an approved client entertainment event matching the criteria in Section 3.

## 5. Approval Limits and Authority
Expense reports are routed automatically in the system based on the total claim value:
* **Claims under $500.00 USD:** Require approval from the employee's immediate Line Manager.
* **Claims from $500.00 USD to $2,500.00 USD:** Require approval from the Line Manager and the Department Head.
* **Claims exceeding $2,500.00 USD:** Require approval from the Line Manager, the Department Head, and the Head of Finance Operations.

## 6. Exceptions and Escalations
* **Late Submissions:** Claims submitted beyond the 30-day limit require a written justification from the employee, endorsement by the Department Head, and final approval from the Chief Financial Officer (CFO).
* **Policy Violations:** Any attempts to submit fraudulent receipts or split claims to bypass approval thresholds will be escalated to the HR Relations Team and Finance Operations Audit Team for disciplinary review.
