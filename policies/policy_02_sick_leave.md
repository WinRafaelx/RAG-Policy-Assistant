# Sick Leave Policy

**Policy ID:** TTB-HR-002  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Owner Department:** People Operations  

## 1. Objective and Scope
This policy defines the provisions and procedures for paid and unpaid sick leave at ttb Policy Assistant Demo Bank. It covers absences due to personal illness, injury, medical appointments, or necessary medical treatments that prevent employees from executing their regular duties.

## 2. Sick Leave Allowance
* **Paid Sick Leave:** All permanent full-time employees are entitled to 15 business days of paid sick leave per calendar year. This allowance is reset on January 1st each year and does not roll over. Unused sick leave is not paid out at the end of the year or upon termination.
* **New Hires:** Employees onboarding mid-year will receive a prorated sick leave allowance calculated at 1.25 days per month of service remaining in the calendar year.

## 3. Reporting and Notification Standards
* **Notification Window:** Employees must notify their immediate Line Manager of their absence due to illness by 08:30 AM on the first day of absence. Notification must be sent via corporate email or the official messaging tool.
* **Details Required:** The notification should indicate the expected duration of the absence. Employees are not required to disclose specific clinical diagnoses but must state if the absence is related to a communicable disease.
* **Failure to Notify:** Unexcused absence without notification by 12:00 PM on the day of absence will be treated as unauthorized unpaid leave and may lead to disciplinary action.

## 4. Medical Certificate Requirements
* **Short-Term Absences:** A medical certificate is not required for sick leave lasting 1 to 3 consecutive business days, unless the employee has been placed on a supervised monitoring program for leave usage.
* **Extended Absences:** A valid medical certificate from a registered healthcare provider is mandatory for any sick leave absence exceeding 3 consecutive business days. The certificate must be uploaded to the People Operations HR Portal within 5 business days of the employee's return.
* **Frequent Absences:** If an employee takes more than 5 separate, uncertified single-day sick leaves in a 6-month period, People Operations may require a medical certificate for all subsequent sick leave requests.

## 5. Transition to Short-Term Disability
* **Exhaustion of Sick Leave:** If an employee remains unfit for work after exhausting their 15 days of paid sick leave, they may apply to transition to the Short-Term Disability (STD) benefit program.
* **STD Compensation:** The STD program pays 75% of the employee's base salary for up to 90 calendar days.
* **Transition Process:** Application for STD requires a detailed medical assessment form completed by a specialist and approved by the People Operations Benefits Team.

## 6. Fit-to-Work Clearance
* **Mandatory Clearance:** Employees returning from a sick leave of more than 10 consecutive business days must obtain a "Fit-to-Work" clearance from a registered medical practitioner.
* **Submission:** The clearance document must be submitted to the People Operations Benefits Team at least 24 hours prior to the employee's scheduled return to duties.

## 7. Escalations and Abuse
Any suspicious patterns of sick leave usage (e.g., consistently calling in sick on Mondays or Fridays) will be investigated. If a Line Manager suspects abuse, they must escalate the case to the HR Relations Team within People Operations. Disputed medical certificates or appeals regarding sick leave eligibility are escalated to the Chief Human Resources Officer for final determination.
